A 55-year-old man operated on for tractional retinal detachment secondary to proliferative diabetic retinopathy, receives topical treatment
including ciprofloxacin eye-drops and ointment. Lost sight of for a month without stopping treatment, patient returns with a real
crystallization of the cornea as central whitish and hard plates corresponding to deposits of fluoroquinolone. The treatment was stopped
immediately and intense lubrication has been prescribed for two days to facilitate debridement of residual débrits. The évolution was marked
by the persistence of a corneal opacification with impact on final visual acuity. The abusive use of fluoroquinolones must be avoided and it
