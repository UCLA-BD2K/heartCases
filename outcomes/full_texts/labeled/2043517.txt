reproduction in any medium, provided the original work is properly cited. Pseudocyst formation, with its attendant complications of
compression, rupture, bleeding and fistula formation, is a well known complication of chronic pancreatitis. In 1966 Berne and Edmondson drew
attention to the often fatal outcome of pancreatico-colonic fistula complicated by hemorrhage. We present two cases of this rare
complication of chronic pancreatitis as defined by the Marseille classification.
