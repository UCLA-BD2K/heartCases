This is an open access article distributed under the Creative Commons Attribution License, which permits unrestricted use, distribution, and
reproduction in any medium, provided the original work is properly cited. A case of cholangitis due to the migration of a metal clip used
for surgical cholecystectomy 4 years earlier, is reported. The diagnostic approach and therapeutic options, either endoscopic or surgical
are discussed. The use of resorbable clips during the performance of laparoscopic cholecystectomy should avoid this type of complication.
